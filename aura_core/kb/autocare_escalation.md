# AutoCare Escalation Ladder (SAE J3016 aligned)

Aura's AutoCare responds to rising driver risk in graded steps rather than jumping straight
to a takeover. Each level maps to an SAE J3016 automation posture and adds interventions.

## Level 0 — MONITORING (green)
Nominal driving. Aura watches the seven-signal fusion against the driver's personal baseline.
Autonomy 0%. No interventions beyond monitoring.

## Level 1 — GENTLE ALERT (blue)
First signs of fatigue. A soft chime or the driver's preferred modality (audio / visual /
haptic). The car keeps full manual control. Confidence required ~55%.

## Level 2 — ACTIVE ASSIST (amber)
Sustained drowsiness. Lane-keeping assist engages, speed eases back, a firmer alert plays.
The car is now actively helping the driver stay safe. Confidence required ~65%.

## Level 3 — PREPARE HANDOVER (orange)
Driver is not responding. Hazard lights begin flashing, windows crack open for airflow, speed
drops toward a safe minimum, and Aura prepares to assume control. Confidence required ~75%.

## Level 4 — SAFE TAKEOVER / PULL OVER (red)
The driver is unfit to continue. The autonomous fallback assumes control, guides the car to
the roadside, brings it to a controlled stop, and can place an emergency call (eCall / 112).
Autonomy 100%. Confidence required ~85%.

## De-escalation and handback
If the driver demonstrably recovers (eyes reopen, responds to a prompt, says "I'm awake"),
Aura de-escalates one level at a time and hands control back gracefully, consistent with a
J3016 handback protocol — never an abrupt all-or-nothing switch.

## Why graded escalation
A single hard threshold either nags or acts too late. The ladder gives the driver every
chance to self-correct at low levels, and reserves the full takeover for genuine
unresponsiveness — which both improves safety and preserves driver trust.
